Florian Ederer

Allen & Kelli Questrom Professor in Markets, Public Policy and Law
Boston University Questrom School of Business

I am an NBER research associate, a CEPR fellow, a member of the Steering Committee for CEPR's Competition Policy RPN, and an ECGI research member.

My research focuses on what drives and hinders innovation, both in and outside organizations, with a particular emphasis on antitrust.

Several of my papers have appeared in top journals including the Journal of Political Economy, Econometrica, and the Review of Economic Studies, have received multiple awards, and have been covered extensively in the popular press. My research has directly shaped antitrust policy at the White House, the Federal Trade Commission, the EU Competition Commission, the UK Competition and Markets Authority, and competition regulators around the world. In 2017, Poets & Quants named me one of the Best 40 Under 40 business school professors in the world.
